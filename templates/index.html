Project



For my final project implementation for CSCI-50 at Harvard, I decided to find a solution to common problems. Due to travel, I've lost a number of plants over the years due to improper watering, especially in the summer. There are a variety of automatic plant waterers on the market, including mechanical solutions. There are also some electric plant waterers, alas these are often too expensive for a basic home use, since these are mostly used in a commercial setting.

Wouldn't it be great to combine different pieces of technology to create a cheap solution? I have decided to combine a microcontroller with wifi capabilities with a soil moisture sensor and a peristaltic pump. The peristaltic pump starts automatically when the soil moisture level is too low, and the soil moisture data is sent to ThingSpeak, an IoT platform from MATHWORKS. The data is available remotely.

This project was designed with a few objectives in mind:

  • Cost effectiveness
  • Ease of use
  • Design flexibility
  • Easily modifiable to implement additional features

Other features can easily be added to this system. Some ideas for future development include:

  • Implementation of multiple soil sensors on a single Arduino to monitor several plants
  • Adding a push feature from the ThingSpeak server to water plant manually with certain security features
  • Collect data regarding latest pump activation times
  • Adding an ambiant air temperature and humidity sensor
  • Collect light exposure data
  • etc.
